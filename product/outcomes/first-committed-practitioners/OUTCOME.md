# First committed practitioners

Solo builders and small product-building teams start using Outcome Engineering as a real working practice for deciding what to build, why it matters, and how product intent should guide agent-assisted implementation.

## Measures

- Builders return across multiple sessions because the graph helps them make better product decisions.
- They can explain the graph structure in their own words after using it on their own product context.
- They use the graph to stop, reshape, validate, or prioritize at least one concrete product decision.
- They keep product intent connected to agent-assisted implementation instead of treating the graph as a separate planning artifact.
- They contribute learning signals that reveal where the practice is valuable, confusing, or missing something important.

## Known / Unknown

- Known: The current strategy targets two ICPs first: solo software product builders and small product-building teams.
- Known: The current strategy is to teach the framework through use, make the graph visually usable, and keep it agent-legible.
- Known: The first user is the builder of Outcome Engineering, so early evidence will come from using the graph to get to an MVP before broad adoption evidence exists.
- Unknown: Which solo builders and small teams feel this pain strongly enough to adopt a repo-native product graph as part of their normal workflow.
- Unknown: What first-session value moment makes the practice worth returning to.
- Unknown: Which community motion, if any, helps committed practitioners emerge.
