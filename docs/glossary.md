# Glossary

## Product Vision

The long-term direction for what the product should become and why it should exist.

## Product Strategy

The choices about where to focus, which customers to serve, which problems to solve, and how the product should win.

## Objective

A qualitative direction for a focused period of work.

## Key Result

A measurable indicator that shows whether progress is being made toward an objective.

## Outcome

A meaningful change in user, customer, or business behavior/state.

## Opportunity

A customer need, pain, desire, context, constraint, or moment of friction that may be worth addressing.

## Solution

A possible intervention intended to address an opportunity.

## Assumption

A belief that must be true for an opportunity to be real or for a solution to work.

## Experiment

A deliberate activity designed to produce evidence about a belief.

## Evidence

Information that changes confidence in a product belief.

Evidence can be qualitative or quantitative. It can come from user conversations, research, observation, experiments, analytics, support, sales, prototypes, technical spikes, tests, or released product behavior.

## Learning

A change in belief caused by evidence.

## Product Decision

A choice about what to pursue, change, stop, or build based on intent, evidence, constraints, and judgment.

## PRD

A product requirements document that captures the chosen product direction for delivery.

## User Story

A description of desired user behavior or capability from the user's perspective.

## Acceptance Criteria

Conditions that must be true for a story to be considered correctly implemented.

## Code

The implementation of selected product behavior.

## Test

An executable check that verifies behavior or implementation.
