# Hosted read-only graph creates repeated alignment value

## Assumption

Small product-building teams get repeated value from a hosted read-only product graph before they need hosted editing, comments, or real-time collaboration.

## Risk Type

Value

## Test

Use the first Outcome Engineering MVP workflow as the initial dogfood test, then repeat with 2 to 3 small founder teams or solo builders who already have a product graph or can review an example graph.

For each participant, share a hosted read-only graph and ask them to use it in at least two product-direction moments: one async review and one live discussion or planning decision. Do not provide editing or commenting features during the test.

## Success / Failure Signal

Success: participants return to the hosted graph without local setup, use it to align on at least one product decision, and can name why read-only access was enough for that moment.

Failure: participants treat the hosted graph as a one-time demo, immediately need editing or comments before it is useful, or continue relying on local files/chats instead of the hosted view.

## Decision It Informs

Whether hosted read-only graph access is a credible MVP step, or whether the product should prioritize local-first onboarding, editing, comments, or another alignment surface before hosting.
