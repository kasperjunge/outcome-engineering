# Vision

A vision is the durable future the product exists to create for the people it serves.

Outcomes prove progress; vision defines the durable future those outcomes should move toward.

Use these five rules when writing or reviewing vision content:

1. Describe the future you want to create.
   Write the long-term destination or desired future state, not the current plan.

2. Keep it customer-centered.
   Say how the product makes life meaningfully better for the people it serves. Avoid internal ambition that customers would not recognize.

3. Make it inspiring but grounded.
   The vision should be ambitious enough to guide and motivate, but plausible enough to shape real decisions.

4. Separate vision from strategy and delivery.
   Vision is the destination. Strategy is the current bet for getting there. Delivery work is the execution. Do not put features, milestones, or short-term plans in the vision.

5. Make it durable and directional.
   A good vision should survive multiple strategies and product iterations while still helping people decide what belongs and what does not.
