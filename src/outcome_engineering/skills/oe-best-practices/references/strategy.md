# Strategy

Strategy is the current bet for moving from vision toward outcomes under real constraints.

Use these five rules when writing or reviewing strategy content:

1. Make a clear choice.
   Say what the product is choosing to focus on now, and what it is not choosing.

2. Name the strategic challenge.
   Respond to a real obstacle, constraint, market condition, customer behavior, or adoption problem. Avoid generic ambition without diagnosis.

3. Define the wedge.
   Say who the strategy serves first, where the product will play, and why that focus gives the product a better chance of progress.

4. Explain how this can win.
   Include the logic of advantage: why this approach could create customer value, business value, learning speed, differentiation, or compounding leverage.

5. Connect vision to outcomes without becoming either.
   Strategy translates the durable vision into a current bet. Outcomes are the changed states that prove whether the bet is working. Do not write outcomes, delivery work, tasks, features, or milestones as strategy.
