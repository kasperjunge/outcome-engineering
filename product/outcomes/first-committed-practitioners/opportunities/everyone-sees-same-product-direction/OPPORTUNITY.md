# I need everyone to see the same product direction without setup

Solo builders, founder pairs, and small teams need an easy way to bring the current product graph into everyday product conversations.

When the graph only lives behind local repo and terminal workflows, it is harder to use as a repeated alignment surface. A founder may understand the graph and keep it updated, but co-founders, collaborators, or external agents cannot casually inspect it and work from the same product direction without help.

This matters because the graph is supposed to keep vision, strategy, outcomes, and current work connected. If people cannot easily see it together, the graph risks becoming private product memory instead of shared product context.

## Evidence

- Hypothesis: "I want my co-founders to see the same product graph I am using, without needing my local setup."
- Hypothesis: "I want to quickly revisit our product direction in a browser when we discuss what matters next."
- Hypothesis: "I want an external agent to understand the current product graph without needing a repo checkout."
- Hypothesis: Shared read access may be enough before shared editing is necessary.

## Known / Unknown

- Known: The current product strategy says the graph should be visually usable and help users stay aligned with the vision.
- Known: The current product strategy says the graph should remain agent-legible.
- Known: The existing graph UI is local and requires running `oe serve`.
- Known: This need applies to the expanded ICP: solo builders, founder pairs, and small teams trying to keep product direction shared.
- Unknown: How often users will return to a read-only hosted graph.
- Unknown: Whether read-only visibility creates enough alignment value before collaboration and editing features are added.
