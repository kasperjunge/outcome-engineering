# Strategy

Outcome Engineering should win first as an agent-native method and toolset that helps builders challenge product thinking while using a repo-native graph as durable product memory.

The core strategic shape is to make Outcome Engineering useful from any messy product entry point, then grow a coherent graph around it. Users should not need to understand the full framework before receiving value. They should be able to bring messy product material, have an agent structure and challenge it, and leave with clearer next steps plus graph artifacts they can keep building on.

## Strategic Pillars

### Challenge assumptions

Users paste messy product thinking and the agent exposes hidden or risky assumptions, helping them avoid building too long without validation.

### Craft vision and strategy

Users work with the agent to clarify the top of the graph: vision, strategy, outcomes, and focus.

### Challenge product intent

Users bring a PRD, spec, issue, idea, or code change, and Outcome Engineering challenges whether it traces upward to opportunity, outcome, strategy, and vision.

## Adoption

The adoption wedge should be agent skills first, backed by the CLI and simple graph files. A lightweight visual graph UI may be important because it makes the invisible structure concrete and exciting.

## Not Now

For now, Outcome Engineering should deprioritize full project management, roadmap planning, analytics dashboards, automatic graph mutation, and perfect graph completeness. Useful partial graphs matter more than exhaustive modeling.

## Near-Term Outcome

People use Outcome Engineering for the three core jobs, feel excited by the visual graph, share it, and some power users begin building out their full product graph.
