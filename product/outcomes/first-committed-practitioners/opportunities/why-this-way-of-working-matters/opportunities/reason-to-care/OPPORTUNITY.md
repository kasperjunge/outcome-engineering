# Small builders need a reason to care

Small product-building teams need to believe Outcome Engineering names a real problem in how they now build. Without that belief, the graph can look like extra process instead of a way to make better product decisions and alignment choices while agents accelerate implementation.

## Evidence

- Hypothesis: Agentic engineering makes implementation faster, which shifts more of the team's constraint toward product judgment, focus, validation, alignment, and maintaining intent.
- Hypothesis: If small builders and teams do not recognize that constraint shift in their own work, Outcome Engineering will feel like extra process.

## Known / Unknown

- Known: The framework is new, so people need a reason to pay attention before they need a workflow.
- Unknown: Which message creates the strongest first belief: faster building creates product drift, small teams need product discipline, agents need product intent, or product work needs a repo-native graph.
- Unknown: What language builders would repeat to describe the problem to someone else.
