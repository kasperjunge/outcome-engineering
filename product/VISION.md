# Vision

Give every product team a living record of why they are building what they are building — so the thread between customer need and working software is never lost.

## The Change We're Making

Most product teams start with genuine insight. They understand customer problems, they have strategic intent, and they make considered bets. But as work moves through the pipeline — from vision to strategy to roadmap to ticket to code — that intent degrades. Customer context lives in Notion. Assumptions live in someone's memory. Evidence lives in a deck that was last opened six months ago. None of it lives with the work.

The result is delivery without memory: teams that ship software they can no longer explain in product terms, discover they built the wrong thing too late, and cannot use learning to update the beliefs that drive their next decisions.

Outcome Engineering treats this as a structural problem, not a discipline problem.

## What We Are Building Toward

A product team using Outcome Engineering can answer two questions at any time:

- **Looking up:** Why does this artifact, decision, or line of code exist?
- **Looking down:** What delivery work and evidence currently support this product intent?

Every product bet is explicit. Every assumption is visible. Every piece of evidence is attached to the belief it bears on. Every agent that works on the product has a context model that keeps it oriented — not just to the task, but to the intent behind the task.

This is not project management. It is a belief management system for product teams that take discovery seriously: teams that know the difference between an output and an outcome, between a synthetic artifact and a grounded one, between a coherent story and a validated one.

## The Product Ideal

Outcome Engineering is the tool a product team would build for itself if it truly believed that:

- Every line of code should be explainable in terms of the customer need it serves.
- Every product bet is a hypothesis until evidence makes it something more.
- Agents and humans can collaborate on product work without collapsing discovery into delivery.
- Learning should update the system, not just live in a retro document.

The graph is the mechanism. Better product judgment — and better products — are the point.
