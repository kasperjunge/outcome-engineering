# First committed practitioners

Solo software product builders start using Outcome Engineering as a real working practice for deciding what to build, why it matters, and how product intent should guide agent-assisted implementation.

## Measures

- A small number of solo builders create product graphs for their own products.
- They return across multiple sessions to refine strategy, outcomes, opportunities, solutions, or assumptions.
- They can explain the graph structure in their own words after using it on their own product context.
- They use the graph to make or change at least one concrete product decision.
- They share examples, questions, feedback, or workflow notes that can seed a practitioner community.

## Known / Unknown

- Known: Agentic engineering makes it faster and easier for individuals to build software products, increasing the need for lightweight product judgment, focus, and validation.
- Known: The current strategy is to teach the framework through use, make the graph visually usable, and keep it agent-legible.
- Unknown: Which solo builders feel this pain strongly enough to adopt a repo-native product graph as part of their normal workflow.
- Unknown: What first-session experience creates enough value that they come back.
- Unknown: Which community motion will work first: public examples, guided teardown sessions, small cohort onboarding, or open-source contribution.
