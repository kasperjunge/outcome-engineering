# ICPs

An ICP is the specific kind of customer, user, buyer, team, or practitioner the product is choosing to serve.

ICPs describe who the graph is for. Outcomes and opportunities use ICPs to stay grounded in a real audience.

Use these five rules when writing or reviewing ICPs:

1. Make the actor concrete.
   Name the kind of person, team, buyer, or organization clearly enough that a reader can tell who is in or out.

2. Describe the lived context.
   Include the situation, workflow, constraints, or environment that makes this group meaningfully different.

3. Connect to the product's wedge.
   Explain why serving this ICP first is strategically useful.

4. Avoid generic segments.
   Do not write broad labels like "users", "teams", "developers", or "companies" unless the surrounding content makes the segment specific.

5. Keep behavior and pain visible.
   The ICP should make later outcomes and opportunities easier to judge, not just list demographics or firmographics.
