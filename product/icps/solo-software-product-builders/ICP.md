# Solo software product builders

## Who They Are

People building software products largely on their own, often with significant help from coding agents. They may be indie hackers, founder-engineers, consultants turning repeated work into a product, or product-minded engineers exploring a market without a full team.

They can now ship faster than they can reliably decide what is worth shipping. Their constraint is less raw engineering capacity and more product judgment, focus, validation, and durable context.

## Jobs / Pains

- Turn messy product thinking into a clear next step.
- Avoid building too much before validating demand, value, and usability.
- Keep vision, strategy, customer understanding, and implementation connected while moving quickly.
- Use agents effectively without letting generated work drift away from product intent.
- Develop enough product discipline without adopting heavyweight team processes.

## Why They Choose Us

- Outcome Engineering gives them a lightweight product operating system that fits a repo-native, agent-assisted workflow.
- They can start from whatever is on their mind, structure it into the graph, and use that graph to decide what to clarify, test, or build next.
- The framework teaches product thinking through use instead of requiring a large upfront methodology.

## Where They Are Not A Fit

- Builders who only want task tracking, project management, or a generic note-taking system.
- Teams that already have a mature product operating model and mainly need enterprise governance.
- People looking for agents to make product judgment for them rather than structure and challenge their own judgment.
