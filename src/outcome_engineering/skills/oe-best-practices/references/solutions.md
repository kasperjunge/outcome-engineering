# Solutions

A solution is an intervention we might build, change, automate, teach, package, or offer to address a specific opportunity.

Opportunities describe customer needs. Solutions describe our intervention. Outcomes prove whether the intervention worked.

Use these five rules when writing or reviewing solutions:

1. Address one clear opportunity.
   The solution should respond to a specific customer need, pain, desire, or want.

2. Stay solution-shaped.
   Describe the intervention. Do not write the customer need, the desired outcome, or delivery tasks as the solution.

3. Name the mechanism.
   Explain why this intervention might affect the opportunity.

4. Reveal the riskiest assumptions.
   A solution should make visible what must be true for it to work. Do not test those assumptions inside the solution; assumption tests belong under the solution.

5. Stay testable before becoming delivery.
   If the solution cannot be tested or de-risked, it is probably too vague.
